2024-25 Season Recap

The 2024-25 Los Angeles Clippers season marked their 55th year in the NBA and their first at the new Intuit Dome in Inglewood. Despite losing Paul George and Russell Westbrook in the offseason and Kawhi Leonard missing the first 34 games due to a knee injury, the Clippers, led by James Harden, achieved a remarkable 50-32 record, securing the 5th seed in the Western Conference. They showcased a strong 18-3 finish in their final 21 games, boasting a top-four defense and high offensive efficiency, with key contributions from Norman Powell, Ivica Zubac, and new additions like Derrick Jones Jr. and Bogdan Bogdanović. However, their playoff run ended in a disappointing first-round exit, losing 3-4 to the Denver Nuggets in a competitive series, highlighted by a Game 7 blowout. (Grok) Read more at NBA.com.

As a Clippers fan, I’m pumped for what’s next! Despite the playoff loss, Kawhi’s leadership and the new arena give us hope. Clippers Nation is ready to cheer louder than ever in 2025-26!
